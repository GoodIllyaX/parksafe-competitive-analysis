04 · Conclusions
📐 Market Standard 1
B2B SaaS = per-camera monthly fee. Everyone charges €15–40/camera/month. Operators expect this model.
📐 Market Standard 2
GDPR compliance is table stakes in EU. "We don't store images" is now a baseline, not a differentiator — unless you add "not even on edge."
📐 Market Standard 3
Consumer apps = pre-booking marketplace (SpotHero). Real-time occupancy in consumer UX is an unsolved problem — nobody does it well.
🎯 MVP Direction
Edge AI + Consumer slot guidance
B2B pays for the infra. Consumer gets the in-garage arrow UX nobody else has. Privacy moat enables both.
⚡ UX Pain 1 — "Ghost spots"
All consumer apps show counts ("12 free") but zero navigation inside the lot. User still wastes 2–3 min finding the actual spot after seeing the number. The last 50 meters are unsolved.
⚡ UX Pain 2 — Trust collapse at night
No app shows safety/lighting data. Users (especially women, BMW M5 owners) choose suboptimal lots due to fear. Community reports exist in SpotAngels but cover only street — garages are a blind spot.
💡 Opportunity 1 — CarPlay + real-time
SpotHero has CarPlay but only for pre-booking. Zero competitors offer real-time occupancy + in-garage arrow guidance via CarPlay. This is Alex's exact job-to-be-done.
💡 Opportunity 2 — B2B privacy unlock
Parkinto and Parquery still send images to cloud (even if deleted). JSON-only edge processing is a legal unlock for CCPA/CA operators (Mike's persona) who currently can't deploy any camera analytics at all.
→ MVP Direction
Edge AI (Jetson) → JSON only → B2B dashboard for 1 operator (Mike) + Consumer CarPlay/map with slot-level arrow guidance (Alex). No competitor does both. Privacy moat makes the B2B sale in CCPA markets. In-garage navigation creates consumer retention that SpotHero and SpotAngels can't replicate without operator data.
